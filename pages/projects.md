This page includes some of the projects that we currently focus on or want to focus on in the foreseeable future. If you are interested in any of these projects, you are very welcome to join us. The output of these projects will be credited to the ELDA.  


<div class="box" markdown="1">

## The Earth-Life Data Catalogue

The [catalogue]({{site.url}}{{site.baseurl}}/catalogue/) is the first priority product of the ELDA. The purpose of the catalogues is to map the available landscape of digital data resources (primarily data) that can be used in the research for the coevolution of Life and other Earth Systems. This catalogue will be developed into a relational database that will be offered to the community as a go-to resource for finding exsiting data sources. The gathered information is intended to be used for recommendations on the better coordination of the global data service, highlighting crucial pitfalls and recommendations. 

</div>

## Failed Large Scale Data Projects

Data integration projects of Earth-Life data entities were initiated in the past decades, and we are aware that many of these have been abandoned after several years. In order to avoid repeating the same patterns, the alliance would like to gather more information about these initiatives so we can analyze the decisions involved and make the community aware of them. 

</div>

## Research White Paper

The currently available data services (especially that involving the fossil record), suffers from shortages of data availability that represents a major obstacle in conducting certain kinds of research. To express the need for data, we would like to present a portfolio of research questions that **cannot be assessed** with the current data provisional service, simply because the necessary data are not available in high enough quality and quantity. This compilation of currently unfeasible research is to be developed into a 'white paper', that we can use to present our needs to the broader scientific community.

</div>

## User requirement specifications of the next generation data service

The ELDA is interested in the general overhaul of the data provisional system for a more integrated, easier and seamless use and sharing of research data - which is ultimately a form of application development. The software development lifecycle starts with the definition of what this upgraded data system must do, itmes that need to be synthesized, organized and prioritized, before any acquisition of funding, or actual implementation of large-scale constructs can take place. We would like the community (data generators, managers, users/researchers) to describe, what they would require from the next generation of data generation systems, in terms access, policy, governance, interface and attribution. 
 
</div>
